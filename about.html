About

Where the engineer meets the priestess.

Chemical engineer by training. Systems thinker by nature. Intuitive by practice. The intersection of those things is where the best brands get built.

I didn't come to branding the traditional way.

I came through chemical engineering — through systems thinking, process optimization, the discipline of understanding how complex things work at a molecular level.

That mind didn't go away when I pivoted into brand strategy. It became the foundation of everything I build. I see patterns other strategists miss. I think in architectures. I approach brand as a system, not a surface.

But systems alone don't create brands that move people. For that, you need something else entirely.

You need intuition.

The ability to listen beneath the words. To sense what a founder is actually trying to say — the thing they haven't found language for yet.

I hold space for both: the precise and the felt. The architectural and the alchemical. The spreadsheet and the ceremony.

That duality is not a contradiction. It's my superpower. And it's what makes the brands I build feel both strategically airtight and deeply, unmistakably alive.

This work is personal.

I've spent years working with founders, healers, coaches, and visionary leaders across health, wellness, and consciousness. I've seen brilliant people do extraordinary work while their brand tells a story that's three versions behind who they actually are.

I know what it feels like to evolve faster than the thing that represents you. To carry something powerful inside and watch the external expression of it fall flat. To know you're undercharging, under-seen, under-valued — not because you lack talent, but because your brand hasn't caught up.

That gap — between who you are and how the world experiences you — is where I work. I close it. Permanently.

What I believe about brand.

01

Identity First

A brand built on identity lasts. A brand built on trends expires. I will always start with who you are before I touch a single visual element.

02

Precision Is Care

Obsessing over details isn't perfectionism — it's devotion. When I'm holding someone's identity in my hands, every choice deserves reverence.

03

Brand Is Ceremony

The act of taking something invisible — your purpose, your gifts — and giving it a form the world can witness. I don't take that lightly.
